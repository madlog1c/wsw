Life Span Mental Health Physical Health
Marine Mammal Science found the average lifespan of captive orcas to be only 12 years.
In the wild, orcas live 30-90+ years
Captive orcas suffer boredom, depression, psychosis, aggression, bullying from other captive orcas, isolation from family, and even total isolation from all other cetaceans (solitary confinement like Kiska).
Captive females are often artificially inseminated at extremely young ages, with little to no family support (all 5 of Kiska’s calves born in captivity have perished).
In the wild these are intelligent, social, family oriented animals, that live their lives together in pods, teaching each other lessons and culture from past generations and the oceans they live in.
All captive male orcas have a collapsed dorsal fin, which is very rare in the wild.
Captive orcas suffer extreme tooth decay and wear (often to the point of losing all their teeth) from grinding on their cages and tanks.
Tiny concrete pools drastically limit (or eliminate) their ability to dive and swim at high speeds.
Captive orcas get sun burns and cataracts from sun exposure due to shallow unnatural water.

Source: SeaWorld Fact Check / Whiting 2015

Mission

WhalesSaveWhales intends on solving the captivity dilemma. The animals need somewhere to go, sea-side sanctuaries need to be built and need ongoing maintenance, and aquariums need to release their tortured captives.

Our first goal is the grow and preserve our treasury. Expenditures will be made in the most thoughtful, effective, and transparent ways possible (via a DAO). Where will a ground-up benevolent decentralized autonomous organization take us?

We envision:

  • A community that spreads knowledge, is engaged in activism for the betterment of animals and our planet, while being kind and helpful
  • Partnerships with relevant artists to enrich the artists, our community, and our cause itself #WhalesSaveWhales
  • A thriving treasury, generating interest and ongoing royalties to sustain healthy expenditures
  • wsw_dao funds internal proposals and outside organizations for specific, actionable, objectives related to our purpose
  • wsw_dao’s “grail” goal is to build (or help build) real world seaside sanctuaries for captive animals, then to empty the tanks into the sanctuaries
  • There will be need for more than one sanctuary, and much work to be done to purchase (or lobby for confiscation) the animals, and physically move them
  • In an optimistic and even further outlook, in a post-captivity world, we may look to create Emergency Rescue Teams to work with wild animals, as well as turning our focus to Ocean Cleanup, clean energy initiatives, etc
